Our History

TechCon began in 2018 as a small gathering of local tech enthusiasts in a community hall. Our founders envisioned a platform where innovative ideas could be shared freely, and where aspiring professionals could connect with industry veterans.

Over the years, TechCon has grown exponentially, moving to larger venues and attracting international speakers and attendees. We pride ourselves on fostering a vibrant community and remaining true to our grassroots origins. Each year brings new challenges and opportunities to push the boundaries of technology together.

From humble beginnings to a renowned international conference, TechCon has always been at the forefront of tech innovation.

Our Mission

Our mission at TechCon is to inspire, educate, and connect the global tech community. We strive to create an inclusive environment where diverse voices are heard, and cutting-edge technologies are showcased. We believe in the power of knowledge sharing and collaboration to drive the future of technology forward.

We are committed to providing accessible content, fostering networking opportunities, and nurturing the next generation of tech leaders. Our goal is to leave attendees not just informed, but truly empowered to innovate.

Notable Past Speakers

Dr. Anya Sharma

Dr. Anya Sharma is a leading expert in Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning. Her groundbreaking work on ethical AI frameworks has influenced policies globally. At TechCon 2022, she delivered a captivating keynote on "AI for Good."

Mr. Ben Carter

A cybersecurity veteran, Mr. Carter has over two decades of experience protecting critical infrastructure. His TechCon 2021 talk on "The Evolving Threat Landscape" was a wake-up call for many organizations. He is known for his pragmatic approach to security solutions.

Ms. Chloe Lee

Ms. Lee is a renowned UX/UI designer and advocate for user-centered design principles. Her work focuses on creating intuitive and accessible digital experiences. At TechCon 2023, she shared insights on "Designing for Empathy in the Digital Age."
